Motivating Example
  • How to estimate average temp 1,000 years ago?
  • Can't measure directly ... no records too
  • Solution: Use indirect evidence

- Can't go back in time - Remote area, no records kept
  • Each year, a tree grows new cells over old ones
  • Creating one light, and one dark growth rings
  • Air temp affects size of growth rings

- New cells form around old cells - Oldest cells in the center, newest at the edge inside the bark - Spring: light rings (cells) - Summer: dark rings (cells) -
  • High temp (usually) leads to larger rings
  • Other factors affect ring size
  • Ring size is observable
  • Temp is hidden

-
What is hidden in Hidden Markov Models (HMM)?
  • Can't measure air temp 1,000 years ago -- Hidden
  • Can inspect tree growth rings formed then -- Observed

Today, we can't measure air temp 1,000 years ago. However, air temp back then affected trees growth. We can see its impact on trees growth by inspecting their growth rings. - Light ring is spring, dark ring is summer. - Number of dark rings = tree age in years
